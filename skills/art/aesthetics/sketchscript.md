# SketchScript Visual Aesthetic System

**Clean hand-drawn sketchnote style that transforms complex ideas into approachable visual summaries.**

---

## Core Concept: Visual Thinking Made Real

A brand aesthetic that embodies the clarity and creativity of sketchnoting—where hand-drawn elements feel intentional, not accidental, and every visual choice reinforces the transformation from spoken word to visual understanding.

**The Philosophy:** *"From conversation to clarity, one sketch at a time."*
- Hand-drawn precision (smooth, intentional lines—not rough sketches)
- Warm, creative workspace feel (not corporate boardroom)
- Visual thinking made accessible (approachable, not intimidating)
- Authentic craftsmanship (clearly hand-made, never flat or digital-first)

---

## The SketchScript Look

### What We Want
- **Clean hand-drawn lines** — Smooth, confident strokes with visible hand quality (like Kalam font in visual form)
- **Warm creative workspace** — Cream backgrounds, natural lighting feel, inviting atmosphere
- **Structured but organic** — Organized layouts with hand-drawn personality
- **Sketchnote DNA** — Visual hierarchy through size, color, and simple illustrations
- **Minimal but warm** — Not cluttered, but never cold or sterile

### Reference Styles
- **Mike Rohde's Sketchnote Handbook** — Clean, structured visual note-taking with clear hierarchy
- **Watercolor sketch illustrations** — Soft edges, warm washes, artistic without being messy
- **Moleskine notebook aesthetic** — Quality paper, authentic materials, creative tools
- **Visual Vocabulary graphics** — Simple icons and symbols that communicate clearly

### What to AVOID
- Corporate blues and sterile grays (too formal, not creative)
- Neon or electric colors (too digital, not warm)
- Flat material design (lacks hand-drawn authenticity)
- Dark mode or dramatic shadows (sketchnotes belong in light spaces)
- Rough/messy sketch lines (we want clean hand-drawn, not rushed)
- Perfect vector precision (needs visible hand quality)
- Rainbow palettes (keep it focused on blue + gold + cream)
- SoN green-teal (#1A6B6B) or burnt orange (#C85A2A) — those belong to Signal Over Noise

## Style Parameters

### Line Quality
Clean hand-drawn strokes with visible human quality. Lines should be smooth and intentional (not shaky or rough), but clearly made by hand rather than vector-perfect. Think: confident marker strokes on quality paper, not whiteboard scribbles.

### Composition Approach
Structured sketchnote layouts with clear visual hierarchy. Use size, color, and spacing to guide the eye. Include simple hand-drawn icons and dividers. Generous whitespace (cream background) prevents visual clutter while maintaining warmth.

### Shadow Treatment
Minimal to none. Prefer subtle borders or slight offset shadows if depth is needed. Sketchnotes live in flat space with hierarchy through size and color, not 3D depth.

### Complexity Level
Balanced simplicity. Include 4-6 key elements per composition with clear visual relationships. Use simple icons and symbols rather than detailed illustrations. Every element should serve the message—no decoration for decoration's sake.
